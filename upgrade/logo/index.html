Logo Design

Creations

Designs 2014-2018

The following pieces are logos I have created throughout the years for commercial businesses, festivals, events, exhibitions, and nonprofits. I enjoy being a creative vessel for these industries. Executing their ideas into visual pieces that represent who they are is truly a rewarding experience.


RGV Taco Truck | 2018

Martin wanted his logo to represent youthfulness and quick customer service. His only visual request was to fit a taco icon he had been using over the years on his truck. To solve these visual problems, I selected orange (because it stimulates appetite) and black for that swift service contrast. A black chunky typeface against a hand-written font best captured the energy we were going for. His icon at last, was incorporated on top. The new logo along with his amazing food has made RGV Taco Truck successfully stand out in a sea of food trucks in Brownsville, Texas.


City of Hidalgo | 2018

The City of Hidalgo hosted a huge Mariachi Festival that invited groups from all over the Rio Grande Valley in a competition to showcase their talent. They needed a logo that was instantly recognizable and true to tradition. I decided to look through vintage Mariachi clothing and was inspired by the golden and shiny embellishments found. So for the logo I decided to incorporate that in the form of a texture. Last but not least, the font was inspired by old movie posters from the Golden Era of Cinema in Mexico that used a lot of Mariachi music.


City of Hidalgo | 2017

Festival of Lights is a Christmas event hosted by State Farm Arena for nearly 30 years. They needed to update their logo with something iconic that everyone instantly recognized and also incorporate a theme that changes every year. To solve this problem, I analyzed the festival for “staple visuals”. I decided to focus on two pillars traditionally found near City Hall for them to use yearly. The fonts needed to stay true to the Christmas Spirit and so a traditional script and a classic serif-font were the perfect fit.


UTRGV | 2015

This logo was done for a friend’s art exhibit that talked about religion throughout the years. She wanted the logo to represent a timeline and something meaningful about humankind and spirituality. This is the most conceptual logo I have ever done but most rewarding. I started a mood board and filled it with futuristic time-based infographics. I liked the circular connotation because it meant wholeness. I also like the number 6 because biblically it implies man's existence sans God, so I used it as little rectangles along the circle for contrast.


State Farm Arena | 2017

Hidalgo’s BorderFest is a festival that celebrates different cultures and traditions from all over the world every spring. 2017 was the year they decided to celebrate our very own country: The United States. The logo needed to be themed and so I gathered all iconic American Landmarks and grouped them together to showcase pride and tradition.


Park Lane | 2014

Park Lane is a logo designed for a 1950’s themed Restaurant. I wanted to capture the audience’s nostalgia by shifting the logo at an angle resembling old candy brands of yesteryear. The retro-inspired typography also helped convey the mood and energy the restaurant conveyed.
